High School

Technical Programs for High School Students

Our high school students choose a technical track alongside their core academics — graduating with both a diploma and a marketable skill.

💻

Web Development

Students learn to design and build websites using modern tools and technologies. From HTML and CSS to interactive design, this program prepares graduates for the growing digital economy in Liberia and beyond.

  • Website design & layout
  • Front-end development
  • Digital skills for employment
⚡

Electricity

A hands-on program covering electrical theory and practical wiring. Students graduate ready to work in construction, maintenance, and electrical services — one of the most in-demand trades in Liberia.

  • Electrical wiring & safety
  • Circuit theory
  • Industrial & residential applications
🧵

Sewing

From basic stitching to full garment construction, the sewing program equips students — especially young women — with skills to launch their own fashion businesses or join the textile industry.

  • Garment making & pattern cutting
  • Fashion design basics
  • Entrepreneurship in textiles
📐

Technical Drawing

Precision, spatial reasoning, and design principles are taught in this program. Graduates pursue careers in architecture, engineering, and construction — fields critical to Liberia's development.

  • Architectural drawing
  • Engineering diagrams
  • Design & measurement

Vocational Training

TVET — Technical & Vocational Education and Training

The TVET program at Don Bosco is a separate track from the high school — open to students who want focused, trade-level skill training. It is a direct pathway to employment and entrepreneurship, providing Liberians with certified vocational qualifications.

TVET programs are fully aligned with national development goals, helping to build Liberia's skilled workforce one graduate at a time.
