Our History

Founded After the War

Don Bosco Technical High School was founded in 1993, in the aftermath of Liberia's devastating civil war. With children left vulnerable and without access to education, the school was established with a clear mission: to take students off the streets and give them quality education — at a fee that would never be a barrier.

From the very beginning, Don Bosco THS has combined academic rigor with technical training, preparing students not just to graduate — but to thrive.

St. John Bosco

Born August 16, 1815 — Becchi, near Turin, Italy

St. John Bosco, affectionately known as Don Bosco, was an Italian Catholic priest who devoted his life to the education and welfare of youth — especially the poor and vulnerable. Raised by his mother, Margaret Bosco, he founded the Salesian Society, which continues his mission worldwide to this day.

Our school proudly carries his name and his legacy.

Our Patron

The Spirit of Don Bosco

Under the Salesians of Don Bosco, our school embodies the values of reason, religion, and loving kindness. We believe in educating the whole student — mind, spirit, and hands — in the tradition Don Bosco established over 150 years ago in Italy.

His model of youth education, rooted in care and practical skill, is exactly what Liberia needed in 1993 — and what our students continue to benefit from today.
